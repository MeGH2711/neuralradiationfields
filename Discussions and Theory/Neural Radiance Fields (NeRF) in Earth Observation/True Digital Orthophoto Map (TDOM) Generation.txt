Traditional Generation of True Digital Orthophotography Maps (TDOMs) mainly depends on Digital Surface Models (DSMs) and complex visibility analysis to handle occlusions. However, this traditional pipeline often produces artifacts like ghosting (disappearance of the subject), geometric distortions, and disappearing edges around building boundaries. To overcome this limitation, frameworks like Ortho-NeRF have been developed, which eliminate the need for explicit geometric priors and traditional occlusion detection \cite{cite6,cite2}.

Ortho-NeRF models complex aerial scenes using a continuous implicit neural representation, and also divides large environments into smaller tiles/patches for better efficient processing. To generate distortion-free maps, this framework uses the true-ortho volume rendering technique instead of standard perspective camera projection \cite{cite6}. Instead of tracing the rays from the central camera viewpoints, this model explicitly casts rays upwards in parallel from normalized ground space. By sampling along these rays through an optimized radiance field, the model inherently resolves complex visibility relationships \cite{cite6,cite2}. This vertical parallel projection removes perspective distortion and helps in reducing the geometric error, holes, and rough building boundaries, which are commonly found in traditional photogrammetry methods.
