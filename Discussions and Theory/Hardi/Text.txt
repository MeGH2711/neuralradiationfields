Section X. Challenges and Research Questions in Remote Sensing.

Solving long-standing challenges in 3D urban modeling has led to a switch from traditional photogrammetric workflow to neural rendering methods like Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS). Literature reviewed in this survey addresses several questions that define the current state-of-the-art.

X.1 Handling Multi-Temporal Radiometric Inconsistency 

Challenge : Remote sensing datasets, particularly those that involve multi-view satellite constellations or multiday UAV missions, are basically multi-temporal [SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance- Adaptive 3DGS]. Basically, images are obtained over vast time scales this leading to major changes in image appearance cause by changes in sun angle, atmospheric conditions, and phenology [SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS, EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. These radiometric changes cause major noise in the neural supervision signal, challenging the multi-view consistency assumption essential for stable optimization [SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS, PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine-Grained Large-Scale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware]. This means that typical models tend to suffer from convergence instabilities where the model is unable to detect surface reflectance from lighting conditions or atmospheric changes[SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS, EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering].

Research Question: How these neural models maintain geometric integrity when the underlying supervision signal shows global and local radiometric changes due to seasonal or atmospheric changes? [SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS]

Proposed Solutions: To maintain geometric and radiometric consistency, neural frameworks make dstinction between static scene geometry from temporary appearance and lighting dynamics [SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS]. Appearance-adaptive latent are empoyed to describe the style of acquisition, with SatGS using a hierarchical model in which global embeddings atthe pre-image level describe broad atmoshepric changes, while local embeddings at the pre-Gaussian level accommodate spectral fine-tuning [SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance- Adaptive 3DGS]. To improve modeling of acquisition-specific sensor responses for example EOGS++ uses learnable affine transformations for each camera, which map the invariant 3D features to colour channels at a specified timestamp [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. Instead of treating shadows as noise S-NeRF and SatGS treat shadows as important information for geometry[SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi-Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS, Shadow Neural Radiance Fields for Multi-view Satellite Photogrammetry]. This utilizes solar visibility fields and surrounding light from the sky to ensure consistency of shadows and buildings [Shadow Neural Radiance Fields for Multi-view Satellite Photogrammetry, SatGS Remote Sensing Novel View Synthesis Using Multi- Temporal Satellite Images with Appearance-Adaptive 3DGS]. Furthermore, in order to improve robustness of the approch against temporary objects llike moving vehicles UPNeRF utilizes the uncertainty and visibility fields this results in variance maps that reduce the loss for unreliable pixles, this prevents degradatio of permanent scene structures [Uncertainty_Guided_Progressive_Few- Shot_Learning_Perception_for_Aerial_View_Synthesis]. X.2 Solving Top-Down Bias in Vertical Reconstruction Challenge: Remote sensing data is predominantly captured from top-down(nadir). This results in “Top-Down Bias” where vertical structures like building facades suffer from significant information loasss and structural ambiguity [Cross-View Geo-Localization via 3D Gaussian Splatting-Based Novel View Synthesis]. this large difference in prespective makes the establishment of geometric correspondences between aerial roof top and facades on the ground level an inherently ill-posed problem[Cross-View Geo-Localization via 3D Gaussian Splatting-Based Novel View Synthesis]. Without the avalibility of sufficient oblique imagery to provide side-view context, neural models often have difficulty disentangling the conflated



foregroung buildings from the background terrain, leading to “jagged” or “sawtooth” effects or complete absence of the vertical elements. RQ: How do neural rendering frameworks solve “Top-Down Bias” inherent in vertical remote sensing imagery to accurately reconstruct vertical buildings surfaces and eliminate geometric features like jagged edges or the merged surface with ground? Proposed Solutions: Neural frameworks leverage explicit geometric control of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) and specialized loss functions to recover high-fidelity vertical structures. To address “Top-Down Bias” limitation in vertical reconstruction this modern frameworks combine oblique and multi-perspective imagery for providing necessary side-view context that missing in predominantly nadir datasets[Comparative Assessment of Neural Radiance Fields and 3D Gaussian Splatting for Point Cloud Generation from UAV Imagery, Enhanced_3- D_Urban_Scene_Reconstruction_and_Point_Cloud_Densification_Using_Gaussian_Splattin g_and_Google_Earth_Imagery]. For example, pipelines like Gaussian Building Mesh (GBM) and Ortho-3DGS utilize the benefits of multiple angular views to recover high-fidelity vertical geometry that is hidden or deformed in standard satlellite-based reconstruction[Gaussian Building Mesh (GBM) Extract a Building’s 3D Mesh with Google Earth and Gaussian Splatting, Ortho- 3DGS_True_Digital_Orthophoto_Generation_From_Unmanned_Aerial_Vehicle_Imagery_U sing_the_Depth-Regulated_3D_Gaussian_Splatting]. This is further improved in cross-view tasks through the use of pseudo-aerial views at a significant pitch angle like 45° or 60° that allow the model to penetrate foliage and capture buiding sides shared by botj ground and aerial views [Cross-View Geo-Localization via 3D Gaussian Splatting-Based Novel View Synthesis]. To complement this data integration, PG-SAG uses semantic-aware scene partitioning to address structural uncertainty between foreground buildings and surrounding background [PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine-Grained LargeScale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware]. By using subgroups for scene partitioning, the model ensures that vertical facades do not overlap with the terrain [PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine-Grained LargeScale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware]. PG-SAG introduce a boundary-aware normal loss to eliminate “jagged” or “sawtooth” effects commonly seen in traditional methods. This loss term reformulates the optimization process along the detected edges in a way that reduce normal vector ambiguity, which ensures better structural preservation[PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine- Grained LargeScale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware]. Lastly, frameworks like GBM also enhance vertical fidelity by applying morphological operations and contour simplification on building mask. This make sures that the building base is correctly define and perventing incomplete masking at the ground-facade interface.



X.3 Massive Data Size Challenge: There are significant hardware challenges in the transitioning from object-centric representations to city-scale digital twin representations due to synchronous optimization of millions of Gaussian primitives over entire urban districts, which eoverreaches traditional hardware’s VRAM limit and this results in prohibitive computational latency[PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine-Grained LargeScale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware]. For example, urban scenes at large scales require large number of Gaussian functions to capture complex details, which can overwhelm GPU memory during both training and real-time rendering [PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine-Grained LargeScale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware, Ortho- 3DGS_True_Digital_Orthophoto_Generation_From_Unmanned_Aerial_Vehicle_Imagery_U sing_the_Depth-Regulated_3D_Gaussian_Splatting] Research Question: How do explicit neural representations scale to city-scale digital twins without overreaching hardware constraints for VRM and/or sacrificing the high rendering speeds required for interactive applications? Proposed Solutions: For addressing these technical challenges, the literature suggests a divide-and-conquer architectural approach that focuses on spatial and semantic partitioning. For scaling neural representations to city-wide environments techniques like Ortho-NeRF apply spatial tile partitioning, where each tile is implicitly rendered and rendered before being stitched together into a coherent True Digital Orthophoto Map(TDOM) based on geographic coordinates[Comparative Assessment of Neural Radiance Fields and 3D Gaussian Splatting for Point Cloud Generation from UAV Imagery]. This strategy is further enhanced by PG- SAG, that utilizes semantic-aware grouping to partition urban scenes into sub-regions based on the visibility checks and hence allows parallel Gaussian optimization [PG-SAG Parallel Gaussian Splatting for Fine-Grained Large-Scale Urban Buildings Reconstruction via Semantic-Aware]. Furthermore, scalability is improved through adaptive resolution control that sets voxel grid resolutions based on target TDOM resolution and the use of multiresolution hash encoding to reduce parameters [Comparative Assessment of Neural Radiance Fields and 3D Gaussian Splatting for Point Cloud Generation from UAV Imagery, Ortho-NeRF generating a true digital orthophoto map using the neural radiance field from unmanned aerial vehicle images]. Lastly, progressive chunking strategies facilitate the reconstruction of ultra-large scenes by aligning chunk boundries to reduce inconsistencies during final merging process [Ortho- 3DGS_True_Digital_Orthophoto_Generation_From_Unmanned_Aerial_Vehicle_Imagery_U sing_the_Depth-Regulated_3D_Gaussian_Splatting].



X.4 Mixing Cameras: Multi-Sensor and Cross-Model Fusion Challenge: Integrating data from multiple satellite platforms is technically demanding because of significant variations in spatial resolutions and sensor characteristics over different missions [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camer Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. Integrating datasets requires complex alignment and radiometric normalization that can introduce noise and reduce structural accuracy. Research Question: How can high-resolution structural information be effectively merged with spectral data from diverse sensors without incurring artifacts and inconsistencies related with traditional preprocessing steps, such as panshapening?[EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering] Proposed Solutions: as indicated by literature, particularly EOGS++ framework that proposes a shift toward pipelines with “virtually zero” required preprocessing by opertaing directly on raw data [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. To avoid spectral artifacts and spatial blurring that is common in exterbal pansharpening, EOGS++ introduces “Direct Rendering” approach [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. This approach works directly with raw high-resolution panchromatic (PAN) images, without the need for low-resolution multispectral data for geometry estimation to maintain maximum amount of structural details [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. This is achieved by duplicating single-channel PAN data to create a three-channel representation and the model optimizes a stable 3D core directly against high-resolution observations [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. Any misalignment caused due to use of different camera types and times stamps is corrected using internal bundle adjustment (BA) embedded directly into training loop [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering]. Utilizing off-the-shelf optical flow estimators like RAFT the framework calculates displacement fields to align rendered views with reference images, thus correcting pre-camera pixel offsets and pose errors without the need for external optimization software [EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct



Panchromatic Rendering]. This integrated approach ensures that structural data from different sensors is geometrically consistent and produces much shaper results than the initializations using raw RPC data[EOGS++ Earth Observation Gaussian Splatting with Internal Camera Refinement and Direct Panchromatic Rendering].
